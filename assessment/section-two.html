Section 2 — Asset Quality

Knowledge Infrastructure & Canon Integrity

This section evaluates whether onboarding knowledge exists as governed operational infrastructure rather than scattered documentation distributed across individuals, folders, or informal communication channels.

In mature onboarding environments, knowledge is organized into a clearly structured system that serves as the single operational reference for onboarding delivery. Documentation is consistently maintained, ownership is defined, and updates occur through structured governance tied to product and process change.

The result is a knowledge system where operators can reliably access accurate guidance, and onboarding quality does not depend on individual memory or interpretation.

This section assesses whether your organization maintains the knowledge infrastructure necessary to support consistent onboarding delivery at scale.

Indicators of high-quality onboarding knowledge assets include:

  • A clearly defined single source of truth for onboarding documentation
  • Structured organization of onboarding knowledge for reliable retrieval
  • Governance triggers tied to product releases and process changes
  • Defined ownership responsible for maintaining canonical documentation integrity

There is a clearly defined single source of truth for onboarding knowledge.

Operators can reliably locate required onboarding knowledge without relying on specific individuals.

A clear owner is responsible for maintaining onboarding documentation accuracy and structure.

Product releases trigger structured review and update of onboarding documentation.

Outdated onboarding materials are identified and retired through a defined process.

Onboarding knowledge remains usable and organized as onboarding complexity increases.

Documentation gaps or inconsistencies are identified before they impact delivery.

Knowledge continuity is maintained when key personnel change roles or leave.

Onboarding documentation sequences activities to support early client-visible value delivery.

Changes to onboarding procedures trigger a defined documentation review process.
